Investigating the Dynamic Small-Scale Coronal Loops

Summer 2025 – Indian Institute of Astrophysics, Bangalore

Supervisor: Dr. Tanmoy Samanta

This study focuses on small-scale coronal loops, a subset of solar campfires, and examines their dynamics using SO/HRI EUV images. We analyze key physical parameters such as footpoint separations, arc lengths, heights, and widths, revealing that these structures are dynamic and short-lived. We suggest that magnetic reconnection is likely a probable cause for their formation. Our findings contribute to a better understanding of coronal dynamics and the role of small-scale loops in solar atmospheric heating.
